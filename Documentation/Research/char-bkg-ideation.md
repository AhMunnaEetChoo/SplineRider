# Character and Background Ideation

Your hook idea is the strongest direction I can see for this game. The mechanics already say: **snap onto a path, build energy, release, arc through space, reconnect**. Making the player a conductive hook turns that from abstract particle physics into something players can read instantly.

**Core Theme**
The player is a small powered hook, clamp, or probe that latches onto energized spline-conductors. While attached, it draws current and converts it into movement. Launching is a controlled discharge. Reattaching is an electrical arc finding the next compatible conductor.

That gives every mechanic a visual meaning:

- Riding: current flowing through the hook.
- Accelerating: brighter charge, longer trail.
- Uphill slowdown: energy being spent against gravity.
- Launching: spark burst / discharge.
- Attachment: snap arc, contact flash, magnetic “lock.”
- Falling: power loss, broken circuit, dimming core.

**What Are The Splines?**
I’d explore three main identities:

1. **Power Cables**
   Curved suspended high-voltage cables, like loose infrastructure in a ruined power grid. This is the most natural fit because cables can be spline-shaped without feeling artificial. They can sag, twist, cross gaps, and glow when active.

2. **Exposed Circuit Traces**
   The whole world is a giant circuit board or machine interior. Splines are glowing copper traces, fiber channels, or soldered conductive paths. This gives you a strong visual language: nodes, capacitors, LEDs, switches, coils, heat vents.

3. **Magnetic Transit Rails**
   The splines are levitating mag-rails or field lines. The player is a magnetized hook/runner pulled along them. This makes speed and snapping feel good, but rails may feel a little more rigid than the fluid Bezier shapes.

My pick: **power cables first**, with some circuit-board motifs in the background.

**Player Character Ideas**
The player should not just be a dot with a hook drawn on it. Give it a readable silhouette:

- **The Sparkhook**: a compact metal hook with a glowing energy core where the handle would be.
- **The Clamp**: a little alligator-clip-like character that bites onto rails and releases with a spark.
- **The Probe**: a sleek electrical test probe with a hooked tip, more sci-fi and precise.
- **The Plug**: a tiny runaway power plug whose prongs hook into conductors.
- **The Arc Rider**: a small charged capsule with a curved grappling horn.

I’d avoid a humanoid character for now. The game’s movement is elegant and abstract; a tool-like character will rotate with tangents more believably and will be easier to animate.

**Background Directions**
The background should explain why these curved conductors exist while staying quiet enough for fast play.

1. **Abandoned Storm Grid**
   Tall pylons, broken transformers, rain haze, distant lightning, hanging cables. Splines are live wires strung through the world. Very readable, atmospheric, and easy to scale from simple to rich.

2. **Inside A Giant Machine**
   The player moves through ducts, coils, generators, and exposed electrical guts. Splines are repair conduits or live rails. Great for level variety: fans, turbines, reactor rooms, battery chambers.

3. **Microscopic Circuit World**
   You are inside a living circuit board. Splines are conductive traces. Backgrounds show chips, solder pads, LEDs, diagnostic markings, oscilloscopes. Clean and iconic, maybe slightly less emotionally rich.

4. **Neon Maintenance Tunnels**
   A sleek arcade direction: black materials, colored rails, glowing hazard strips, distant city infrastructure. Strong readability, but risks feeling generic unless the hook character is charming.

My favorite is **Abandoned Storm Grid** because gravity, falling, dangling cables, and electrical danger all belong there.

**Reference Lanes**
Not to copy directly, but useful as mood targets:

- **Tron: Legacy** for luminous paths, dark negative space, and speed trails.
- **Teslagrad** for electricity/magnetism as a game-world language.
- **N++** for high-contrast minimalist movement readability.
- **Celeste** for how small characters can feel responsive through particles, squash, and trails.
- **INSIDE / Limbo** for side-on silhouette clarity, though I’d make Spline Rider more colorful and energetic.
- **Hyper Light Drifter / Transistor** for saturated glow accents against restrained environments.

**Art Direction I’d Choose**
Make it **electric salvage sci-fi**: not clean cyberpunk, not medieval fantasy, not cute mobile-cartoon. Think dark industrial spaces, teal/cyan live cables, amber sparks, red hazard elements, and occasional hot white electrical bursts.

Palette idea:

- Active spline: cyan / electric blue.
- Player core: warm yellow-white, so it stands apart from the spline.
- Launch sparks: orange-white.
- Goal: stable green or gold power node.
- Danger/death: red-orange overload.
- Background: deep charcoal, desaturated steel, muted storm blues.

**A Strong First Visual Target**
First screen: a tiny glowing hook clings to a sagging live cable between ruined pylons. Far behind it, a storm-lit power station flickers. The next spline hums faintly across a gap. When the player launches, the hook spits sparks and arcs toward the next conductor.

That gives the game a clear promise: **you are not just riding lines; you are completing broken circuits at speed.**
